Introduction

With the continuous development of the Internet to meet the growth of smart cities and the upcoming 4th industrial revolution, Internet of Things, Cloud Computing, Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, and Big Data are increasingly being integrated into the fabric of our works and lives alongside mobile and social media. These technologies pose challenges to industries and organisations to adapt and to transform. The purpose of this class is to provide a framework for students to understand the context of this development, so they can equip themselves with required skills to prepare for a career in the field.

As smart, connected, and data driven products and services become more prevalent in this coming decade, knowing the sources of data and how to acquire them programmatically for further cleaning, filtering, aggregation, modelling, evaluation, visualisation, and on-demand interaction will become increasingly important. The class will focus on the UX/UI aspect of data driven product and service design with the goal of producing a product requirement document (PRD), informed by qualitative and quantitative user research and front-end prototypes.

Course Objectives

  • Use Design Thinking and Computational Thinking constructs to frame and solve user experience problems found in new media products and services.
  • Use of Customer Journey Map (CJM), Data Flow Diagram (DFD), and Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) for preparing a Product Requirement Document (PRD) in new media product and service development.
  • Understand basic UX, visual communication, data encoding and human-computer interactions (HCI) principles and how that affect the user experience in consuming new media products and services.
  • Master the fundamentals of web development, including basic coding in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, and SQL.
  • Use digital tools such as ParseHub, Open Refine, Google Sheet, and Airtable to acquire, filter, clean, transform, and visualise data throughout the CRISP-DM data mining process.
  • Leverage API, IoT, web scrapping, open data and popular JavaScript based data visualization libraries to develop web applications.
  • Integrate front-end web development with back-end database and Content Management System (CMS) using RESTful API/JSON as the data transfer vehicle.
  • Use Figma for visual design and prototyping of data intensive dashboard app.
  • Apply Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager, Google Optimize and UTM coding in conducting A/B test and conversion analysis.
  • Consolidate learning through building a data driven web app complemented with usability studies and analytics tracking.
